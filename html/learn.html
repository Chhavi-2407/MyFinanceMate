Budgeting Basics

4 lessons

Start Here
1. What is a Budget?

A budget is a plan for your money. It helps you track what comes in (income) and what goes out (expenses). Think of it as a roadmap for your financial journey!

2. The 50/30/20 Rule

This simple rule suggests: 50% of income for needs (rent, food), 30% for wants (entertainment, dining out), and 20% for savings and debt repayment. It's a great starting point!

3. Tracking Your Expenses

Write down everything you spend for one month. You'll be surprised where your money goes! Use apps, spreadsheets, or even a notebook. Awareness is the first step to control.

4. Creating Your First Budget

List your monthly income, then list all expenses. Subtract expenses from income. If it's negative, cut some wants. If positive, increase savings! Review and adjust monthly.

Smart Saving

4 lessons

Essential
1. Why Save Money?

Savings provide security for emergencies, help you reach goals, and reduce financial stress. Even small amounts grow over time with compound interest!

2. Emergency Fund Basics

An emergency fund is money set aside for unexpected expenses (medical bills, car repairs). Aim for 3-6 months of living expenses. Start with $500, then build gradually.

3. Savings Goals & Strategies

Set specific goals (vacation in 6 months, laptop in 1 year). Break them into monthly targets. Automate transfers to savings on payday. Pay yourself first!

4. High-Yield Savings Accounts

These accounts offer higher interest rates than regular savings accounts. Your money grows faster! Compare rates online and choose FDIC-insured banks for safety.

Investing 101

5 lessons

Grow Wealth
1. What is Investing?

Investing means putting your money into assets (stocks, bonds, real estate) that can grow in value over time. Unlike savings, investments carry risk but offer higher potential returns.

2. Stocks vs. Bonds

Stocks are shares of companies. You own a tiny piece! Bonds are loans to companies/governments. Stocks are riskier but grow faster. Bonds are safer but grow slower. Diversify!

3. Understanding Risk & Return

Higher potential returns usually mean higher risk. Young investors can take more risk because they have time to recover from losses. As you age, shift to safer investments.

4. Index Funds & Mutual Funds

These funds pool money from many investors to buy a mix of stocks/bonds. They're diversified and managed by professionals. Great for beginners! Lower risk than individual stocks.

5. Starting Small with SIPs

Credit & Debt

3 lessons

Important
1. Understanding Credit Scores

Your credit score (300-850) shows how reliable you are at repaying debt. Higher scores = better loan rates. Pay bills on time, keep credit card balances low, and check your score regularly.

2. Good Debt vs. Bad Debt

Good debt helps you build wealth (student loans, mortgage). Bad debt comes from overspending on wants (high-interest credit cards). Avoid bad debt and manage good debt wisely!

3. Credit Card Basics

Credit cards are convenient but dangerous if misused. Pay the full balance every month to avoid interest. Never spend more than you can repay. Treat it like a debit card!

Financial Protection

3 lessons

Safety First
1. Why You Need Insurance

Insurance protects you from big financial losses (medical emergencies, accidents). Pay a small premium regularly instead of a huge bill suddenly. Health insurance is essential!

2. Avoiding Financial Scams

Never share passwords or OTPs. Verify before clicking links. If something sounds too good to be true, it probably is. Research before investing. Protect your financial information!

3. Identity Theft Prevention

Use strong, unique passwords. Enable two-factor authentication. Monitor bank statements regularly. Shred financial documents before discarding. Be cautious with personal information online.
